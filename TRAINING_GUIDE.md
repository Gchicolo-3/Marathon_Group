# Marathon Pipeline Engine — Your Guide

Written for Michael Sullivan. No tech background needed. Ten minutes to read, and you'll know everything you need to run this thing.

---

## What is this?

It's a prospecting machine that works for you every weekday morning while you're having coffee.

It finds facilities and capital-projects people at hospitals and private schools in NJ, NY, CT, and PA. It scores them against your ideal client profile. It writes outreach emails in your voice, signed with your name. And it watches the news for reasons to reach out — a new capital project, a bond approval, an expansion.

The one thing it never does is send an email without you. Every email waits for your approval. You're the last word, always.

Your dashboard: **https://marathon-crm-dashboard.vercel.app**

Log in with the password you were given. Works on your phone, iPad, or computer.

## What happens automatically every morning (7am ET, weekdays)

Step by step, this is the machine doing its rounds:

1. **The Prospector** searches the live web for real, named people who fit your ideal client — VPs of Facilities, Directors of Capital Projects, CFOs — at healthcare and education organizations in the tri-state area. It skips organizations already in your pipeline, so you don't see repeats.
2. **The Qualifier** scores each new prospect 1–10 against your ideal client profile. Sixes and up move forward; the rest get set aside so you never waste time on bad fits.
3. **The Copywriter** writes a short, direct intro email for each qualified prospect — your voice, your phrasing, signed "Michael Sullivan, Marathon Group LLC." Those drafts go into your approval queue.
4. **Follow-ups**: anyone you emailed 7+ days ago who hasn't replied gets the next email in the 26-week campaign drafted — also into your queue, never sent on its own.
5. **The News Agent** checks the news on companies in your pipeline for trigger events — capital projects, funding approvals, expansions, leadership changes. Finds show up in the **Signals** tab.
6. **At 8am**, the system writes your **Today's Brief** — a plain-English summary at the top of the dashboard telling you exactly what needs you today.

## How to use the dashboard

**The homepage** — Today's Brief up top tells you what needs your attention. Below it, four numbers: prospects in the system, emails waiting for your approval, active email sequences, and news signals this week. Then the pipeline board — every prospect as a card you can drag between stages (New → Qualified → Contacted → Replied → Meeting set → Won/Lost).

**Approval queue** — this is your daily stop. Each pending email shows up one at a time. Read it. Edit anything you want, right in the text box. Then either **Approve & next** (it moves on to the next one), **Regenerate** (the AI takes another swing), or **Skip** (deal with it later). When you approve and then hit **Send** on a deal, it opens the email in your own mail program — you press the actual send button.

**Signals** — the news agent's finds. Each card shows the organization, what happened, and why it matters to you. This is gold for openers: "Saw the bond approval for the new tower…" beats any cold intro.

**Activity** — the running log of everything: every agent run, every draft, every note, every stage change, newest first. If you ever wonder "did this thing do anything today?" — this is proof of work, one glance.

**Companies / Contacts** — your rolodex. Click into anyone to see or edit details.

**Runs** — a technical log of each morning's runs. Green "ok" means it ran clean. You rarely need this page.

## What YOU need to do each day

Almost nothing. Here's the whole routine:

1. Open the dashboard with your coffee.
2. Read Today's Brief — 15 seconds.
3. Open the approval queue. Read each draft, tweak if you want, approve.
4. Glance at Signals for anything worth a phone call instead of an email.

That's it. Five to ten minutes. Reply handling is still you — when a prospect writes back, that's your relationship to run. The machine gets you to the conversation; you take it from there.

## FAQ

**What if I see a bad draft?**
Fix it or toss it. You can edit the text directly and approve your version, or hit **Regenerate** for a new take, or **Skip** it entirely. Nothing goes out unless you approve it. And if a draft is bad in a way that keeps repeating — wrong tone, wrong angle — say so; the writing instructions can be tuned.

**What if I want to add someone manually?**
Go for it — the machine doesn't get jealous. On the pipeline board, hit **New deal** and enter the person and company. Once they're in, everything works the same: hit "Score with AI" on their page to qualify them, and "Generate with AI" to draft their email.

**What if nothing ran today?**
First: it only runs weekdays — no runs Saturday or Sunday. Check the **Runs** tab; if the morning shows red "error" rows or nothing at all, something hiccuped. One bad morning is no crisis — it picks back up the next day, and nothing is lost. If it's red two days in a row, flag it to George.

**Does it ever email anyone without me?**
No. Every email — first touch and every follow-up — sits in your queue until you approve it, and sending happens from your own mail program.

**Why did a prospect disappear from the pipeline?**
It probably scored below 6 against your ideal client profile and was moved to Lost. Nothing is deleted — filter the board or check Companies to find anyone.

**Where do the prospects come from? Are they real?**
The Prospector searches the live web each morning for named people in current public sources — not made-up names. Still, titles change and people move, so a quick LinkedIn glance before an important send is never wasted.
